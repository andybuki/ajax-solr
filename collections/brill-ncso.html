North China Standard Online (1919-1927)

CONTENT: Founded in late 1919, the North China Standard started as a semi-official organ of the Japanese Ministry of Foreign Affairs. In the highly competitive English-language news market, it aimed at counteracting negative opinions against the Japanese, but gives proof of "real" journalist\'s research of "real" news. Being mainly distributed in Tianjin and Beijing it enjoyed a wide readership in China and Japan. It ceased publication in 1930; the database covers the years 1919 to 1927 using the holdings of the Waseda University and a private collection as its basis.
