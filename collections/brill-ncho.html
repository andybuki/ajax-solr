North China Herald

CONTENT: Published in Shanghai on a weekly basis from 1850 to 1940s, the North China Herald counts as the foreign newspaper press in China with the longest history. Despite its main base being Shanghai the newspaper had correspondents across the whole of China and thus contains relevant national and local news on an extensive range of topics, as well as gossip reflecting the life of the foreign settlements. Its daily pendant is the North China Daily News whose history started a bit later but continued after a war time break up until 1951.
